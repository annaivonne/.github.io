I am a Visiting Scholar at the University of Texas at Dallas and received my PhD in Accounting from Fudan University. My research focuses on intelligent accounting, management accounting, financial reporting, and the applications of data analytics and machine learning in accounting and corporate finance. I have presented at major conferences including the AAA Annual Meeting, Accounting and Business Research, and the UTD Finance Research Conference.

My recent work examines the interactions between intelligent accounting, financial statement analysis, and management accounting to improve corporate decision-making and governance. I also contribute to projects exploring innovation disclosure, corporate governance, and capital market efficiency. I have received multiple honors, including the Fudan University Outstanding Student Scholarship and the Fudan Doctoral Student Forum Third Prize.

At Fudan University and UTD, I teach financial accounting, management accounting, financial statement analysis, and intelligent accounting applications. I bring strong technical expertise to my research, with proficiency in Python (including deep learning), STATA, SAS, ChatGPT, DeepSeek, and advanced data analysis. I am skilled in designing empirical research and communicating complex accounting and finance concepts to diverse audiences.
