This study examines the effectiveness of the STAR Board's listing standards in identifying firms with innovation potential and optimizing resource allocation. The findings indicate that companies meeting the innovation attribute criteria outperform those meeting only earnings attribute criteria in innovation after IPO. Compared with firms listed on other boards, firms listed on the STAR Market that only meet earnings attribute criteria benefit from the institutional support, but do not exhibit significantly higher innovation investment, indicating a misallocation of resources.
